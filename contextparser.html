HTML 5 Context Parser

Our novel approach to enforce HTML 5 parsing across the browsers


Contextual Analysis

In order to prevent XSS vulnerabilities, we need to address two big challenges.

We have to understand all output contexts as defined in the HTML 5 specification to perform the contextual analysis and apply the context-dependent escaping filters.
Browsers are NOT fully compliant with HTML 5, and can interpret the resulted HTML in a way inconsistent to our compliant contextual analysis. This issue could make our filters ineffective in escaping characters that are sensitive to the misaligned context.

Context Parser & HTML 5 Compliance

Context Parser is our underlying component to interpret the execution contexts of HTML templates based on the HTML 5 specification. It largely focuses on the Tokenization process only, instead of implementing the end-to-end model of the whole HTML 5 parser, to make our codebase tiny and speedy.

To address the cross-browser compatibility issues, we introduce a template pre-processor called "Canonicalization". In this process, it automatically rewrite the HTML templates to correct any potential parse errors. This ensures that the resulted templates can be consistently interpreted by all supported browsers based on the HTML 5 specification. Downstream components and applications can thus focus on the HTML 5 contextual analysis and escaping without worrying about cross-browser compatibility issues.
